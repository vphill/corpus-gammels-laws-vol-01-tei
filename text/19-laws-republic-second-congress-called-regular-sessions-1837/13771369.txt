JOINT RESOLUTION
For the relief of John Buchanan.
Be it resolved, by the senate and house of representatives of the
republic of Texas, in congress assembled, That the sum of one hun-
dred and sixty dollars shall be, and the same is hereby required to
be paid out of any moneys in the treasury not otherwise appro-
priated, to John Buchanan, for his services as secretary rendered
the government, ad interim; and the auditor is hereby required to
allow the said claim, of the said John Buchanan, and give a draft
therefor, whenever the same may be presented for the amount men-
tioned in this resolution.
JOSEPH ROWE,
Speaker of the house of representatives.
MIRABEAU B. LAMAR,
President of the senate.
Approved, Dec. 9, 1837.
SAM. HOUSTON.
JOINT RESOLUTION
For the relief of John M. Clifton.
Resolved, by the senate and house of representatives of the re-
public of Texas in congress assembled, that the first auditor be
instructed to audit the accounts of J. M. Clifton, for fourteen hun-
dred and thirty-four dollars, thirty-nine cents for his expenses in
enlisting and mustering into the service of Texas ninety-seven men
in the spring of eighteen hundred and thirty-seven.
JOSEPH ROWE,
Speaker of the house of representatives.
MIRABEAU B. LAMAR,
President of the senate.
Approved, Dec. 9, 1837.
SAM. HOUSTON.
