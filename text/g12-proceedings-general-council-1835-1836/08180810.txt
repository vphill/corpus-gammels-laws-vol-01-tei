Council Hall, WASHINGTON, )
March 4th, 1836.	5
NINE O’CLOCK, A. M.
The Council met pursuant to adjournment.
A committee from the Convention (in session at this place) consist-
ing of Messrs. Parmer, Houston and Coleman, came in, and through
Mr. Parmer verbally informed the Council that the convention had as-
sembled and were organized.
To which the president replied verbally, “that the members of the
council were ready to deliver their archives to any government that
might be established by the convention, or to any person authorized by
that body to receive them, and begged the committee to return thanks
to the Convention for their attention, &c.
A quorum not being present.
On motion the House adjourned till nine o’clock to-morrow morning.
