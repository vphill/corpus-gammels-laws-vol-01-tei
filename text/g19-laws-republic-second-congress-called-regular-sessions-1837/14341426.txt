AN ACT
To define the boundaries of the county of Liberty.
Sec. 1. Be it enacted, by the senate and house of representa-
tives of the republic of Texas, in congress assembled, That the
territory included in the following limits shall constitute and com-
pose the county of Liberty: Beginning on the Gulf of Mexico at the
southwestern corner of the county of Jefferson, thence north along
the western boundary line of said county to the Big Sandy creek,
(thence down said creek with said county line, to its entrance into
the Neches river,) thence up said river to the southeastern corner
of the county of Houston, thence south of west along the southern
boundary line of said county to the Trinity river, thence across the
river in the same direction to a point nine miles distant, thence in
a direct line to the head of Cedar Bayou, thence down said Bayou
to its entrance into the Gulf of Mexico, thence along said Gulf with
all its meanderings, including the Trinity Bay to the place of be-
ginning.
JOSEPH ROWE,
Speaker of the house of representatives.
S. H. EVERITT,
President pro tem. of the senate.
Approved, Dec. 18, 1837.
SAM. HOUSTON.
